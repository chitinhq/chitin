# Soul archetype survey — cross-domain cut (da Vinci lens)

The audit question: are the 8 canonical souls (Curie, Knuth, da Vinci,
Shannon, Lovelace, Socrates, Sun Tzu, Turing) the right set, before we
invest in automated ELO scoring? My slice is cross-domain — what do
*other* fields use for cognitive specialization, and what does that
suggest chitin is over- or under-fitting?

## 1. Hypothesis (written before the searches)

Three priors, declared up front so I can score them after:

- **H1 — Chitin is over-indexed on the "thinking" axis.** All 8
  canonical souls map to research/engineering thought modes. I expect
  external frameworks to give substantial weight to a *social /
  coordination / facilitation* role that chitin treats as either Sun
  Tzu's secondary duty or no one's. Belbin's "Co-ordinator" and
  "Team Worker" roles, Holacracy's Facilitator, jazz's drummer-as-
  groove-setter — none of these have a clean chitin analogue.
- **H2 — Chitin is under-indexed on the "execution under failure"
  axis.** Canonical set is biased toward design and analysis (Curie,
  Knuth, Lovelace, Shannon, Socrates, Turing all sharpen specs or
  measurements; Sun Tzu routes; da Vinci sketches). The "stay alive
  while everything is on fire" lens is in *experimental* (Hamilton),
  not canonical. Surgical CRM and Apollo flight software take this
  role as load-bearing.
- **H3 — 8 is high but not unusual.** Belbin = 9, Jung/Pearson = 12,
  D&D 4e = 4 + variants, MOBA = 5–6, Final Fantasy = 87 jobs but
  ~6–8 archetypes underneath, Big Five/HEXACO = 5–6 dimensions, jazz
  rhythm section = 3 roles. The cluster of "5 to 12" suggests 8 is
  defensible on volume; the question is *which 8*, not *how many*.

## 2. Method

Frameworks searched (12, sources cited in section 3):

1. Belbin Team Roles (9-role workplace taxonomy)
2. Big Five / HEXACO (lexical-trait personality dimensions)
3. Jungian / Pearson 12 archetypes (narrative + brand)
4. Campbell's Hero's Journey (single-role arc, not taxonomy)
5. D&D 4e/5e class roles (Striker/Defender/Controller/Leader)
6. Final Fantasy job system (87 jobs across 3 meta-categories)
7. MOBA / League of Legends class system (Tank, Marksman, Controller,
   Fighter, Slayer, Mage + position roles)
8. Spotify squad / tribe / chapter / guild
9. Holacracy circle-and-role governance
10. Surgical OR team + Crew Resource Management (CRM)
11. US Army mission command vs detailed command doctrine
12. Jazz ensemble rhythm-section roles
13. Kuhn's "essential tension" (traditionalist ↔ innovator dialectic)

Skipped (delegated or low-yield prior):

- Agent-framework taxonomies (Sun Tzu's slice).
- Generative-art / DSL ensembles (Lovelace's slice).
- Sports positions beyond MOBA — basketball is already represented as
  a single experimental soul (Jokić); the position taxonomy doesn't
  generalize cleanly to cognition.
- IPIP-NEO and 16PF — lexical descendants of Big Five, redundant
  signal.
- Spiral Dynamics, MBTI — known to have weak empirical support;
  including would dilute the table.

Method bias to flag: every framework I picked has *some* external
corroboration (academic, doctrinal, or commercial). I deliberately
excluded mystical/typology systems with no measurement. That's a
da Vinci move (observation over dogma) but it means the table is
biased toward formalized taxonomies — which may itself be a finding
about chitin's stance.

## 4. Findings

Three patterns surfaced. Each has named evidence; none is a
recommendation.

### F1 — Every framework with > 4 canonical roles distinguishes "produces work" from "protects the conditions for work"; chitin only has the first.

- **Belbin** splits Plant (idea generator) from Team Worker (smooths
  interpersonal friction) and Resource Investigator (external
  scouting).
- **D&D 4e** distinguishes Striker (deals damage) from Leader (heals
  + buffs) — explicitly different roles, not the same role at low
  HP.
- **Surgical OR** has Circulating Nurse as a separate, codified role
  whose job is non-sterile environment management — *the room*, not
  *the patient*.
- **Jazz** treats Bass and Drums as equal-billing rhythm-section
  roles; the soloist is replaceable, the rhythm section is not.

In chitin's canonical 8: Curie measures, Knuth proves, Lovelace
generates, Shannon decodes, Socrates challenges, Sun Tzu routes,
Turing formalizes, da Vinci sketches. **Eight ways to produce work,
zero ways to protect the conditions.** Hamilton (incident
stabilization) and Hopper (cleanup / pruning) and arguably Jokić
(orchestration without scoring) all live in *experimental*. The
external evidence consistently treats this category as load-bearing,
not optional.

The closest canonical analogue is Sun Tzu, but Sun Tzu *routes*
work — that's a third category (selection / prioritization), not
protection. Routing chooses which work to do; protection keeps the
already-running work alive. They're different.

### F2 — Chitin treats "the answer is one soul" as the default; every domain that performs under stress treats role-blending and role-swapping as the default.

- **Surgical CRM** explicitly trains *all* team members to speak up
  across hierarchy when they see a problem — the role boundaries
  become permeable under stress, by design.
- **Jazz** trades fours: the same human is soloist for 4 bars, comper
  for 4 bars, repeatedly. Role is per-bar.
- **Final Fantasy V / Tactics** lets a character carry abilities
  across jobs — the Red Mage is structurally a blend, not a
  half-of-each.
- **MOBA position-vs-class** is a 2D grid: a Tank can play any of 5
  positions; the position changes the role's expression.
- **Holacracy** explicitly says one person fills many roles, and
  governance can rewire the role-set live.
- **Kuhn** insists the *same* scientist must be both traditionalist
  and innovator — splitting them into two people loses the synthesis.

Chitin's quorum picks *one* soul per phase (e.g., the 2026-04-19 vote
gave Phase B to Knuth, period). The ELO file tracks each soul as a
distinct competitor. The current PR-level switching cadence
(da Vinci → Curie → Knuth across this week) is closer to the
external pattern than the formal model suggests, but the model
doesn't *name* blends or hand-offs as first-class. Every external
framework that operates under real consequences treats "the
boundaries are permeable" as a feature, not as a degenerate case.

### F3 — Chitin has invented something genuinely novel that no surveyed framework has: per-soul "scope notes" that bind a lens to a specific live decision.

Reading the canonical files, the *scope notes* on Curie ("Curie is
active **for the Phase B restart of the dogfood-debt-ledger plan
only**") and Knuth ("Knuth is active **for the Phase B finish**")
are unlike anything in the surveyed set. Belbin assigns you a role
for life. D&D class is permanent. Holacracy roles are stable across
weeks. Even the Final Fantasy class-change is a character-level
event, not a project-level one.

Chitin is doing **per-decision lens activation** with explicit scope
windows and explicit handoffs (Curie → Knuth at Phase B handoff).
This pattern — *short-lived, scoped, named, handoff-coupled
activations of a cognitive lens* — appears nowhere in the
literature I surveyed. The closest analogue is jazz's per-piece
bandleader assignment, but even that is per-set, not per-decision.

This is either:

- **A real innovation** worth defending and naming as a first-class
  primitive (the "scope note," the "hand-off vote," the "strike
  log") — in which case the canonical 8 is too small a question, and
  the right question is "what infrastructure makes scoped activation
  legible to the rest of the swarm?"
- **An aspirational pattern that won't survive contact with
  automation.** When ELO is automated, scope-bound activations will
  be hard to score (which window counts? what's the control?
  Curie's note explicitly *names* a hypothesis — does the next
  Curie session inherit that or restart?). Curie's own heuristics
  suggest the ELO framework will demand the kind of cleanly-bounded
  trials that scope-notes blur.

Either way, this isn't covered by the external taxonomy and the
re-quorum should look at it directly.

## 5. Open questions for the re-quorum

Specific, named, non-prescriptive — these are questions, not
recommendations.

1. **The protection role.** Belbin, D&D, surgical CRM, and jazz all
   treat "protect the conditions for work" as a distinct role.
   Hamilton and Hopper sit in experimental. Should one of them be
   canonical, or is there a third soul (not yet named) that
   captures *protective review* — the lens whose job is "this PR
   ships and survives Friday afternoon"? See F1.

2. **The facilitator role.** Belbin's Team Worker and Holacracy's
   Facilitator have no chitin analogue. Is this because (a) the
   user *is* the facilitator and souls don't need one, or (b)
   chitin has a missing role and inter-soul friction will surface
   it later? The current ELO scoreboard's "user-curated, opinion-
   weighted" framing implicitly puts the user in the facilitator
   seat — should that role be promoted to a soul, or is making it
   a soul a category error?

3. **The generalist.** Final Fantasy's Red Mage and Belbin's lack
   of a "generalist" slot represent two different design choices.
   Chitin currently has *no* canonical generalist — every soul is
   sharp. Is da Vinci the generalist (cross-domain implies broad)
   or is da Vinci a specialist in *analogy*, leaving an unfilled
   generalist slot? The strike against da Vinci on PR #19 may have
   been a generalist failure mode (claimed to observe broadly,
   actually skipped a specific contract check).

4. **The continuous-axis question.** Big Five and HEXACO represent
   personality as N continuous dimensions, not discrete classes.
   Chitin's souls are discrete identities. Is there value in
   surfacing the *axes* underneath the canonical 8 — e.g.,
   "rigor × speed," "convergent × divergent," "design × execution"
   — as named dimensions, with each soul being a position in that
   space? This would change what ELO scores: not "which soul won
   today" but "where in trait-space did today's good work land?"

5. **The role-blending question.** Every external framework that
   performs under real consequences allows mid-task role
   transitions (jazz trading fours, surgical CRM speak-up, MOBA
   in-game class flex). Chitin's quorum picks one soul per phase.
   Should "soul A handed off to soul B at moment X" be a
   first-class event, with its own ELO consequences for the
   *transition* rather than just the soul? F2 evidence suggests
   the lack is a real gap, not a stylistic choice.

6. **The Jester.** Pearson's 12 archetypes have a Jester whose
   specific cognitive function is "dissolve false seriousness,
   break recursive abstraction loops." Chitin has zero playful
   souls — every one is earnest. Socrates *interrogates*, but
   doesn't *deflate*. Is this an oversight, or did the absence
   reflect a deliberate decision (and if so, where is that decision
   recorded)?

7. **The novelty of scope notes.** Per F3 — chitin's scope-note
   pattern appears to be genuinely novel. Should the re-quorum
   audit the *infrastructure around* the souls (scope notes, ELO
   deltas, strike logs, handoff votes) before re-auditing the
   souls themselves? The canonical 8 may be the wrong layer to
   re-evaluate; the activation protocol may be where the leverage
   is.

8. **The Kuhn question.** Should *tension* (paired adversarial
   souls, held simultaneously) be promotable to a first-class
   concept? Socrates ↔ Sun Tzu is named in Socrates' file as "the
   adversarial pair partner," but no other pair is. If pair-tension
   is load-bearing in the way Kuhn argued, the unit of canonization
   may be the *pair*, not the individual.

## Hypothesis check (post-research)

- **H1 (over-indexed on thinking, missing facilitator)** —
  *supported by 3 frameworks* (Belbin, Holacracy, surgical CRM).
  See F1 and Q2.
- **H2 (under-indexed on execution-under-failure)** — *supported
  by 4 frameworks* (D&D Defender role, surgical CRM,
  Apollo/Hamilton, jazz rhythm section as foundation). Hamilton
  and Hopper are experimental — F1 and Q1.
- **H3 (8 is defensible on volume, the question is which 8)** —
  *supported* — the cluster is 5 to 12 across surveyed frameworks;
  8 is not anomalous. The composition is the open question.

One unexpected finding (F3, scope notes) was not in any prior
hypothesis and is the most interesting result.

## Decision-rule paragraph (what's missing, what's unique)

External evidence treats two roles as load-bearing that chitin's
canonical 8 lacks: a **protection / conditions-keeper** lens
(Hamilton-shaped; D&D Leader, surgical Anesthesiologist,
jazz drummer) and a **facilitator / friction-smoother** lens
(Belbin Team Worker, Holacracy Facilitator) — Hamilton sits in
experimental, no facilitator exists at all. Conversely, chitin has
explicitly rejected the **continuous-axis** representation that
Big Five / HEXACO formalize (every soul is a discrete identity, not
a coordinate) and the **role-blending under stress** that surgical
CRM and jazz trading-fours both treat as default; chitin instead
activates one soul per phase via quorum vote. The genuinely novel
chitin move with no external analogue is the **scoped, dated,
handoff-coupled activation** pattern (Curie's "Phase B restart only"
note, Knuth's "Phase B finish only" note, the explicit return-to-
da Vinci instruction) — this is denser than any external taxonomy's
binding of role to context, and may be where the actual research
investment belongs rather than in re-curating the canonical set.
